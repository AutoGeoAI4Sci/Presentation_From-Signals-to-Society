## 📌 Event Overview

**From Signals to Society** is a data science seminar hosted by the  
**Texas A&M Institute of Data Science (TAMIDS)**.

As a TAMU Data Science Ambassador, I am excited to co-organize this seminar with Wenjing Gong and Weishan Bai to foster interdisciplinary dialogue across data science, geography, and urban analytics at Texas A&M University.

The event explores how advanced data science methods — from signal processing to GeoAI — can help decode complex Earth systems and human behavioral dynamics.

This seminar brings together interdisciplinary research at the intersection of:

- 🌍 Geospatial Science  
- 📡 Signal Processing & Seismic Sensing  
- 🧠 Human Behavior Modeling  
- 🌡 Climate & Environmental Analytics  
- 🏙 Urban & Regional Science  
- 🤖 AI & Data-Driven Decision Systems

## 🎤 Speakers & Topics

### 🧠 Hao Tian  
**Department of Geography, Texas A&M University**

**Talk:**  
*Can Seismic Signals Decode Human Behavior? Toward a Data-Driven Understanding of Human Dynamics*

This talk explores how passive seismic sensing combined with data-driven modeling can decode patterns of human activity and mobility — particularly under environmental stress and extreme weather conditions. The research bridges physical sensing systems with computational analytics to uncover hidden behavioral signals embedded in environmental data streams.

---

### 🌡 Yining Liu  
**Urban & Regional Science, Texas A&M University**

**Talk:**  
*Outdoor Thermal Comfort and Recreational Walking Thresholds Among Older Adults*

This presentation examines how outdoor thermal conditions influence walking behavior among older adults. By integrating real-world activity data with environmental analytics, the research contributes to urban resilience, climate adaptation, and health equity research.

## 🎯 Seminar Objectives

This event aims to:

- Demonstrate how raw environmental signals can be transformed into societal insight  
- Showcase interdisciplinary GeoAI and data science applications  
- Highlight emerging methods in spatial–temporal modeling  
- Connect environmental sensing with human behavioral response  
- Strengthen the data science community at TAMU
