# 📌 Event Overview

**From Signals to Society** is a data science seminar hosted by the  
**Texas A&M Institute of Data Science (TAMIDS)**.

As a **TAMU Data Science Ambassador**, **Yifan Yang** co-organized this seminar with **Wenjing Gong** and **Weishan Bai** to foster interdisciplinary dialogue across data science, geography, and urban analytics at **Texas A&M University**.

The seminar explores how **environmental signals, sensing technologies, and data-driven modeling** can be transformed into insights about **human behavior, environmental systems, and urban dynamics**.

By bridging **physical sensing systems with advanced data science and GeoAI methods**, the event highlights emerging approaches to understanding complex **Earth–human systems**.

# 🎤 Speakers & Topics

## 🧠 Hao Tian  
**Department of Geography, Texas A&M University**

<p align="center">
<img src="./picture/hao tian.jpg" width="480">
</p>

### Talk  
**Can Seismic Signals Decode Human Behavior?  
Toward a Data-Driven Understanding of Human Dynamics**

This talk explores how **passive seismic sensing combined with data-driven modeling** can decode patterns of **human activity and mobility**, particularly under environmental stress and extreme weather conditions.

The research bridges **environmental sensing infrastructure and computational analytics**, demonstrating how seismic signals can serve as indirect indicators of human behavioral dynamics embedded within environmental data streams.

## 🌡 Yining Liu  
**Urban & Regional Science, Texas A&M University**

<p align="center">
<img src="./picture/yining liu.jpg" width="480">
</p>

### Talk  
**Outdoor Thermal Comfort and Recreational Walking Thresholds Among Older Adults**

This presentation investigates how **outdoor thermal conditions influence recreational walking behavior among older adults**.

By integrating **real-world activity data with environmental analytics**, the study contributes to research on:

- Urban resilience  
- Climate adaptation  
- Healthy aging  
- Climate-health interactions

The work provides insights for **climate-responsive urban planning and public health policy**.

# 🎯 Seminar Objectives

This seminar aims to:

- Transform **raw environmental signals into actionable societal insights**
- Demonstrate interdisciplinary applications of **GeoAI and spatial data science**
- Highlight emerging approaches in **spatiotemporal modeling**
- Connect **environmental sensing technologies with human behavioral responses**
- Strengthen collaboration across **data science, geography, and urban science**
